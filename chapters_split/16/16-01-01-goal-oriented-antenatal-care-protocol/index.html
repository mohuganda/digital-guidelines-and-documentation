    Chapter 16: Obstetric Conditions¶

    16.1.1 Goal-Oriented Antenatal Care Protocol¶

    Important: Goals are different depending on the timing of the visit. 4 visits are aimed for in an uncomplicated pregnancy. If a woman books later than in first trimester, preceding goals should be combined and attended to. At all visits address any identified problems, check the BP and measure the Symphysio-Fundal Height (SFH). All women must receive Hb, HIV testing and Syphilis testing (RPR) routinely.

    Timing Of Visit

    Any time before 14 weeks gestation

    BOOKING VISIT

    Develop birth and emergency plan

    Give health education

    Check foetal growth and maternal well-being

    Start preventive interventions

    Identify and manage any illness

    Plan for ANC

    Patient assessment

    Goals

    LMP Confirm period of gestation

    Access for SGBV

    Family history

    STI

    Contraceptive use (type, duration)

    Obstetric

    Surgical

    Medical

    History Taking

    SFH (symphysio-fundal height) Abdominal exam Vulva exam (speculum if indicated

    Vital observations (BP, Pulse rate, respiratory rate)

    General exam including evidence of trauma and mood,

    Examination

    If RDT/BS positive, follow guidelines Check for hereditary conditions if suspected sickling test G6PD)

    If Mother has fever (Temp above 37.5oC do RDT/ BS

    Blood grouping

    HBsAg testing

    Hb estimation

    If BP > 140/90, check urine for protein

    Urinalysis (Urine strip & microscopy)

    HIV test

    Syphilis test (RPR)

    24-28 weeks

    Check for pregnancy-induced hypertension (PIH)

    Determine foetal growth and movement

    Exclude anaemia

    Exclude multiple pregnancy

    Give TT

    Goals

    Ask for any social problems and illnesses Ask date of first foetal movements Ask if there is/ was any vaginal bleeding or discharge

    Social support

    History Taking

    Abdominal exam: rule out multiple pregnancy

    Measure SFH

    Check for Oedema and Pallor

    Measure BP and weight

    For Rhesus Negative mothers, do anti body screening

    Viral load/CD4 for HIV positive mothers

    Repeat HIV test
