# Chapter 14: Gynecological Conditions

Primary dysmenorrhoea occurs more commonly among adolescents and
young women. Symptoms usually begin 6–12 months after menarche
and occur mainly with ovulatory cycles. Generally, severity of symptoms
decreases with age, sexual activity and child birth.

Clinical features

Lower abdominal cramping

Backache, headache

Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, fainting, fever, fatigue, dizziness

Differential diagnosis

Endometriosis

Other causes of lower abdominal pain
