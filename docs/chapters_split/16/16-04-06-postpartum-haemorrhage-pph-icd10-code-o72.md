# Chapter 16: Obstetric Conditions

## 16.4.6 Postpartum Haemorrhage (PPH) ICD10 CODE: O72

Vaginal bleeding of more than 500 mL after vaginal delivery or >1000
mL after caesarean section.

after delivery
PPH is an EMERGENCY. It can occur in any woman and needs prompt
recognition and treatment.
Causes
 Tone: failure of uterus to contract, precipitated labour

- Primary PPH occurs in the first 24 hours after delivery

- Secondary PPH occurs between 24 hours and six weeks

 Tissues: such as retained placenta (in part or whole) or
membranes which may lead to atony as well as infection in
the uterus

 Thrombotic disorders which may be due to DIC following
abruptio placenta or severe APH
High risk patients
 History of previous PPH, multiple previous C/S, multiple
pregnancy
 Placenta praevia, abruptio placenta
 Precipitated labour, prolonged labour, large baby

 Do not give heat stable carbetocin for treatment of

Prevention
 Ensure active management of 3rd stage of labour for all
women in labour, and delivery by skilled staff

 Give heat stable Carbetocin 100mcg IV/IM (single dose) or
oxytocin 10 IU IM or misoprostol 600mcg orally to the
mother within 1 minute of delivery, after ruling out presence
of another baby
 Clamp the cord and cut it (3-5 minutes after birth) or when
the cord stops pulsating.
 Controlled cord traction during a contraction with counter-traction to deliver the placenta
 Massage the uterus immediately after delivery of the placenta
to ensure the uterus is contracted
 Identify mothers at risk and manage accordingly
