Obstetric Fistula

ICD10 CODE: 071

CHAPTER 16: Obstetric Conditions

Obstetric fistula is an abnormal communication between the birth canal,
and either the bladder, ureters, or rectum. It is one of the major causes
of maternal morbidity making the women with the condition suffer from
constant urinary incontinence which can lead to skin infections, kidney
disorder or death if left untreated.
Causes
¬ç Obstructed labour (main cause): most fistula develops in 2
weeks after an obstructed labour, causing an often expansive
crush injury to the vaginal tissues
¬ç Sexual abuse and rape (Gender-based violence)
¬ç Complication of unsafe abortion
¬ç Surgical trauma usually following a caesarean section
¬ç Gynaecological cancers and radiotheraphy

Predisposing factors
¬ç Lack of access to maternity care
¬ç Lack of/inadequate skilled care at birth
¬ç Lack of facilities for ANC and childbirth
¬ç Lack of knowledge to identify danger signs and promptly
respond
¬ç Poverty and lack of women empowerment

¬ç Early marriage and childbirth

¬ç Harmful traditional practices such as Female Genital Mutilation
Clinical features
¬ç Unncontrolled leakage of urine or faeces from vagina
Differential diagnosis

¬ç Inadequate family planning access

¬ç Stress, urge or overflow incontinence
¬ç Ureterovaginal fistula (UVF)
