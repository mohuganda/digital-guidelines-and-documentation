# Chapter 16: Obstetric Conditions

## 16.4.4 Ruptured Uterus

ICD10 CODE: O71.1

Partial or complete tearing of the uterus, common in: ¬ç Multiparous women (i.e. have had >1 live babies) ¬ç Women with previous caesarean section

Causes/predisposing factors ¬ç Assisted deliveries/obstetric procedures ¬ç Neglected obstructed labour ¬ç Tearing of a poorly-healed uterine scar during labour ¬ç Short interpregnancy interval of less than 18 months after

Caeserean Section

¬ç Previous history of uterine surgery, e.g. myomectomy

¬ç Damage to uterus due to a blow, e.g. kick or accident ¬ç Use of oxytocic herbs

Clinical features ¬ç Cessation of regular uterine contractions (labour pains) ¬ç Continuous abdominal pain ¬ç Vaginal bleeding ¬ç Anxiety, anaemia, and shock ¬ç Abdomen is irregular in shape ¬ç Foetal parts easily felt under the skin if the foetusis outside

uterus and foetal heart is not heard Differential diagnosis ¬ç Abruptio placentae ¬ç Placenta praevia ¬ç Other causes of acute abdomen in late pregnancy ¬ç Ruptured spleen ¬ç Bowel obstruction

Investigations ¬Å Blood: CBC, grouping and cross-matching Management

Mothers with a suspicion of ruptured uterus should be referred immediately to hospital for blood transfusion and surgical management.
