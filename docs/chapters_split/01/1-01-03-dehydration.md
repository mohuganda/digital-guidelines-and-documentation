# Chapter 1: Emergencies and Trauma

## 1.1.3 Dehydration

A condition brought about by the loss of significant quantities of fluids and salts from the body.

Causes

- Vomiting and/or diarrhoea

- Decreased fluid intake

- Excessive loss of fluids, e.g. due to polyuria in diabetes,

excessive sweating as in high fever, burns Clinical features

- Apathy, sunken eyes/fontanel, loss of skin turgor (especially

in children)

- Hypotension, tachycardia, deep (acidotic) breathing, dry mu-

cosae, poor or no urine output. 1.1.3.1 Dehydration in Children under 5 years Assess degree of dehydration following the table below: Clinical features of dehydration in children
