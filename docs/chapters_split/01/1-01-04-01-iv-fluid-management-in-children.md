# Chapter 1: Emergencies and Trauma

## 1.1.4.1 IV Fluid management in children

ICD10 CODE: E87.8

|TREATMENT|LOC|
|---|---|
| Total daily maintenance fluid requirement is 100 ml/kg for the first 10 kg plus<br><br>- 50 ml/kg for the next 10 kg plus 25 ml/kg for each subsequent kg<br><br> Give more than above if child is dehydrated or in fluid loss or fever (10% more for each 1°C of fever)|HC4|

Shock in non-malnourished child

|TREATMENT|LOC|
|---|---|
| Use Ringer’s lactate or normal saline  Infuse 20 ml/kg as rapidly as possible|HC3|
|If no improvement  Repeat 10-20 ml/kg of IV fluids  If bleeding, give blood at 20 ml/kg If still no improvement  Give another 20 ml/kg of IV fluids If no improvement further still  Suspect septic shock  Repeat 20 ml/kg IV fluids and consider adrenaline or<br><br>dopamine<br><br>If improvement noted at any stage (reducing heart rate, increase in blood pressure and pulse volume, capillary refill <2 seconds)<br><br> Give 70 ml/kg of Ringer’s lactate (or Normal saline if Ringer’s not available) over 5 hours (if infant <12 months) or 2.5 hours (if child >12 months)|HC4|
|Note  In children with suspected malaria or anaemia with shock, IV fluids<br><br>should be administered cautiously and blood should be used in severe anaemia|Note  In children with suspected malaria or anaemia with shock, IV fluids<br><br>should be administered cautiously and blood should be used in severe anaemia|
