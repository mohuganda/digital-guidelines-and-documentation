# Chapter 1: Emergencies and Trauma

## 1.1.3.2 Dehydration in Older Children and Adults

Assess degree of dehydration following the table below.

### Clinical features by degree of dehydration

| Clinical feature | Degree of dehydration Mild | Moderate | Severe |
|---|---|---|---|
| General appearance | Thirsty, alert | Thirsty, irritable | Confused, anxious, drowsy, coma |
| Pulse | Normal | Rapid | Rapid, thready, sometimes absent |
| Respiration | Normal | Deep, may be rapid | Deep and rapid |
| Systolic BP | Normal | Normal | Low, may be immeasurable |
| Skin pinch | Rapidly returns | Returns very slowly | Returns very slowly |
| Eyes | Normal | Sunken | Very sunken |
| Tears | Present | Absent | Absent |
| Mucous membranes | Moist | Dry | Very dry |
| Urine output | Normal | Reduced | Anuria, empty bladder |

!!! note "Note"
    At least 2 of these signs must be present.

### Management

| Treatment | LOC |
|---|---|
| Mild dehydration | HC2 |
| Give oral ORS 25 ml/kg in the first 4 hours | |
| Moderate dehydration | |
| Give oral ORS 50 ml/kg in the first 4 hours | |
| Severe dehydration | HC3 |
| If there is shock use Normal Saline 0.9%, 50 ml/kg in the first 4 hours | |
| Give IV fluids rapidly until radial pulse can be felt, then adjust rates | |
| Re-evaluate vital signs every 30 minutes during the first few hours | |

| Time period | Volume of fluid |
|---|---|
| First 1 hour | 1 L |
| Next 3 hours | 2 L |
| Next 20 hours | 3 L |

- After 4 hours, evaluate hydration in terms of clinical signs, **not** in terms of volume of fluid given.
- As soon as signs of dehydration have disappeared but not before, start fluid maintenance therapy, alternating ORS and water to avoid hypernatraemia if one is the patient wants.
- Continue for as long as the cause of the original dehydration persists.

!!! note "Note"
    Volumes shown are guidelines only. If necessary, volumes can be increased or initial high rate of administration maintained if signs of dehydration persist.

!!! caution "Caution"
    Avoid artificially sweetened juices.

### Prevention (for all age groups)

Encourage prompt use of ORS at home if the personis
vomiting and/or having diarrhoea.
