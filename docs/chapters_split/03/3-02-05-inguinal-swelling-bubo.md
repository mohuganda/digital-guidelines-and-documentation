# Chapter 3: HIV/AIDS and Sexually Transmitted Infections

## 3.2.5 Inguinal Swelling (Bubo)

It is an STI syndrome presenting as localised swellings or enlarged lymph glands in the groin and femoral area.

Causes

- Chlamydia strains: lymphogranuloma venerium (LGV)

- Heamophilus ducreyi: chancroid

- Treponema pallidum: syphilis Clinical features

- Excessively swollen inguinal glands

- Pain, tenderness

- Swellings may become fluctuant if pus forms Differential diagnosis

- Other causes of swollen inguinal lymph nodes, e.g. leg ul-

- Obstructed inguinal hernia Investigations
