# Chapter 17: Childhood Illness

## 17.2.2 Check for Jaundice
If jaundice present, Ask

|If jaundice present, Ask

- When did the<br><br>jaundice appear first?|Look and feel

- Look for jaundice (yellow<br><br>eyes or skin)<br><br>

- Look at the young infant’s palms and soles. Are they yellow?|
|---|---|

Classify jaundice as in the following table

|SIGNS|CLASSIFY AS|TREATMENT|
|---|---|---|
|

- Any jaundice if age less than 24 hours or<br><br>

- yellow palms and soles at any age|Severe Jaudice| Treat to prevent low blood sugar (breastfeed or give expressed breast milk or sugar water by cup or NGT)<br><br> Refer urgently to hospital<br><br> Advise mother to keep infant warm .<br><br> Advise mother to give home care for the young infant<br><br> Advise mother to return immediately if palms and soles appear yellow<br><br> If the young infant is older than 14 days refer to hospital for assessment|

|SIGNS|CLASSIFY AS|TREATMENT|
|---|---|---|
|

- Palms and soles not yellow|Jaundice| FOLLOW UP in one day:<br><br>- If palms and soles are yellow, refer to hospital<br>- If palms and soles are not yellow but jaundice has not decreased, advise FOLLOW UP in one day<br>- If jaundice is decreasing, reassure mother and<br>- FOLLOW UP in two weeks<br>- If still there in 2 weeks, refer to hospital<br>|
|
