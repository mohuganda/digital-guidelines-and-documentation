# Chapter 22: Skin Diseases

## 22.5.2 Urticaria/Papular Urticari

ICD10 CODE: L50

An acute, sub-acute or chronic inflammation of the skin, caused by
endogenous or exogenous agents. Urticaria is an itchy skin rash.
Causes

Endogenous: familial, also associated with other allergic diseases

Exogenous: agents include sunlight, chemicals, certain foods,
insect bites

Clinical features

Inflammation of skin: transient itching hives and wheals

Papular urticaria: vesicles, redness, oedema, oozing in case
insect bites
