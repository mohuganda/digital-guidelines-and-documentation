# Chapter 23: Oral and Dental Conditions

## 23.3.2 Herpes Infections

ICD10 CODE: B00

Infections caused by virus herpes (simplex and zoster)
Causes

Both simplex and zoster infections can affect the face and oral
cavity

Herpes simplex: cluster of painful vesicles around the mouth
(cold sores or fever blisters). Can be recurrent

Herpes zoster: multiple small vesicles (2-3 mm) that ulcerate and
coalesce to form larger ulcers on the oral mucosa

- Commonly on the vermillion border, gingiva, dorsal tongue,

and hard palate

- Always present as a unilateral lesion and never cross the
- midline
- Pre-eruption pain followed by the development of painful
