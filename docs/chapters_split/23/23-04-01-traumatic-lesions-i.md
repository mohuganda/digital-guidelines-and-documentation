# Chapter 23: Oral and Dental Conditions

## 23.4.1 Traumatic lesions I

ICD10 CODE: S00.5

|TYPE OF LESION|FEATURES|
|---|---|
|Fibroepithelial polyp<br><br>Over-vigorous response to low grade recurrent trauma resulting in fibrous hyperplasia|

- Well-localized sessile or pedunculated lump, usually located on the palate or lateral surface of the tongue|
|Mucocele<br><br>Saliva extravasation into the tissues from damage to minor salivary gland ducts. They are commonly seen in the lower labial and ventral lingual mucosa|

- History of trauma and characteristic appearance|
|Ranula<br><br>A mucocele that occurs from the sublingual gland|

- Blue, transparent sublingual swelling|

Management

|TREATMENT|LOC|
|---|---|
|Fibroepithelial polyp  Excision biopsy and histological confirmation Mucocele  Surgical removal (recurrence may occur if there is regular<br><br>trauma) Ranula  Excision of the sublingual gland|RR|
