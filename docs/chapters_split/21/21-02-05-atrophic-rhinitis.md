# Chapter 21: Ear, Nose & Throat Conditions

## 21.2.5 Atrophic Rhinitis

Chronic infection of the nasal mucosa in which various components
become thinner (atrophy) due to fibrosis of the terminal blood vessels.
Cause

Unknown but associated with: HIV/AIDS, poor socio- economic
status, syphilis, rhinoscleroma (early stages)

Clinical features

Tends to affect both nasal cavities

Affects females more than males

Foul stench not noticed by patient who cannot smell

Crusts and bleeding points in the nose

Epistaxis when crusts separate

Sensation of obstruction in the nose

Nasal airway very wide

Investigations
 C&S of smear of nasal material
 X-ray: To exclude sinusitis
 Differential diagnosis
 Atrophy from other causes
