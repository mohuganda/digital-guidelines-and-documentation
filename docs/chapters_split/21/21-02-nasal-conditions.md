# Chapter 21: Ear, Nose & Throat Conditions

## 21.2 NASAL CONDITIONS

- Paper, foam rubber (e.g., mattress foam)

- Beads, stones, metal objects Clinical features

- Usually inserted by the child, and therefore mostly found in the

right-hand nasal cavity

- Foreign body noticed by child/parent  May be visible or felt  Sharp object may cause bleeding

- Unilateral foul-smelling discharge from the nose Differential diagnosis

- Infection in the nose, sinuses, or adenoids Investigations  Usually not required (Clinical diagnosis is enough)  X-rays may be helpful in case of metallic objects like wires or

ball bearings
