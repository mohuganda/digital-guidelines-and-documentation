# Chapter 21: Ear, Nose & Throat Conditions

## 21.2.2 Epistaxis (Nose Bleeding)

ICD10 CODE: R04.0

trait/disease, renal failure, often familial

- Can also be a symptom of serious disease, e.g., typhoid, malaria,

viral fevers such as Ebola Clinical features

- On examination, site of bleeding from nose may be seen

- Signs and symptoms of shock if bleeding is severe

- Signs and symptoms of predisposing cause Differential diagnosis

- Clinical assessment to exclude any of above causes Investigations  Blood: Full blood count, platelet count

############ Management

|TREATMENT|LOC|
|---|---|
|First Aid  Sit the patient up (if the patient is not in shock) and tilt<br><br>head forward not backwards to avoid pooling of blood in posterior pharynx<br><br> Instruct patient to pinch the nose between the finger and the thumb for 15 minutes, breathe through the mouth, and spit out any blood|HC2|
|If bleeding continues  Impregnate a gauze strip with Soft paraffin or Tetracy-<br><br>cline eye ointment and pack into the nose using forceps<br><br> Leave gauze in place for 24-48 hours If bleeding still does not stop after this period  Refer to hospital for further management| |

Prevention

- Avoid picking the nose

- Treat/control predisposing conditions
