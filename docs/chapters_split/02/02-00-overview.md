# Chapter 2: Infectious Diseases

Anthrax is an acute zoonotic infectious disease caused by the bacterium
Bacillus anthracis. It most commonly occurs in wild and domestic animals,
such as cattle, sheep, goats, camels, antelopes, and other herbivores.
B. anthracis spores can live in the soil for many years.

Clinical features

Symptoms vary depending on how the disease was contracted, and
usually occur within 7 days
