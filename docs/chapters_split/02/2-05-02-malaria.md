# Chapter 2: Infectious Diseases

## 2.5.2 Malaria

ICD10 CODE: B50

Malaria is an acute febrile illness caused by infection with Plasmodium
parasites and is transmitted from person to person by an infected female
anopheles mosquito.

 There are five Plasmodium species of malaria parasites which
infect humans namely: P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. ovale, P. malariae
and P. knowlesi.
 P. falciparum is the most virulent and also the most common
malaria parasite in Uganda.
