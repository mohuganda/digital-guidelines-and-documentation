# Chapter 6: Gastrointestinal and Hepatic Diseases

## 6.1.2 Acute Pancreatitis

ICD10 CODE: K85

Acute inflammation of the pancreas.

CHAPTER 6: Gastrointestinal and Hepatic Diseases

TREATMENT

Cause

Excessive alcohol intake

Gall stones, biliary tract disease (obstructive cancer or anatomical abnormalities)

Infections, e.g. mumps, HIV, hepatitis A, ascaris

Drugs, e.g. sulphonamides, furosemide, lamivudine, analgesics, organosphosphate poisoning

Clinical features

Acute abdominal pain usually in the epigastrium radiating
to the back

Pain worsened by eating or lying down and relieved by
sitting up or leaning forward

Nausea, vomiting, abdominal distension

Fever, tachycardia, dehydration (may be severely ill with
shock)

Abdomen is very tender but in the absence of peritonitis
there is no rigidity/rebound tenderness

Complications

Pseudocysts

Peritonitis

Differential diagnosis

Perforated peptic ulcer, peritonitis

Acute cholecystitis, inflammation of the biliary tract

Sickle-cell anaemia crisis
