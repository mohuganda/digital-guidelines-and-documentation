CHAPTER 5: Respiratory Diseases

Most anti-TB drugs can cause hypersensitisation between week 3 and
week 8 of treatment in order of frequency: ethambutol, pyrazinamide,
rifampicin and isoniazid.
If mild (simple itchy rash), give antihistamine (e.g. chlorpheniramine) and
moisturizer and continue treatment.
Severe reactions are characterised by
- Fever, headache, vomiting
- Macular dark erythematous rash which can progress to a
Steven Johnson-Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis syndrome
