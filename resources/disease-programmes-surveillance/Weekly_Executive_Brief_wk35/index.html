    MINISTRY OF HEALTH, UGANDA¶

    Weekly Executive Brief¶

    Week 35 | 28th Aug - 3rd Sep 2022

    This brief provides an overview of; OPD attendance, notifiable diseases, other diseases of public health interest and causes of death.

    OVERALL

    Compared with week 34 , OPD attendance, TB cases and Reporting rates declined. However, maternal deaths, neonatal deaths, malaria incidence, measles cases, Severe Acute Respiratory Infections (SARI) cases and malaria related deaths increased in the reporting week. The reporting rate was 85.7%.

    OPD ATTENDANCE

    The number of people who visited OPD decreased by

    7% i.e.. (from 674,803 in week 34 to 625,077 in the

    current week 35). There has been a general downward trend in OPD attendance between week 29 and week 35.

    STATUS OF MATERNAL DEATHS

    Maternal Deaths by district-Week-29

    23 maternal deaths were recorded– more than the number (19) reported in week 34. Maternal deaths were reported in 15 districts. Kampala reported 5 and Hoima-3 deaths while Kabarole and Mbarara each reported 2. Agago, Adjumani,

    Bushenyi, Iganga, Kibaale, Kyankwanzi, Mayuge, Mbale Wakiso, etc. districts each reported one maternal death.

    UPDATE ON EARLY NEONATAL DEATHS (0-7 Days)

    The number of early neonatal deaths increased by one from 121 in week 34 to 122 in the current reporting week.

    In week 35, early neonatal deaths were reported in 37 out of 136 districts with over 60% (77/122) of deaths reported in Kabale District & Kampala, Masaka, Lira, and Mbarara cities. The rest of the districts each reported between 1 and 4 neonatal deaths during the week.

    Week34 Week35

    Week 35 | 28th Aug - 3rd Sep 2022

    MALARIA CASES, INCIDENCE AND DEATHS¶

    Incidence per 1,000 Population at risk– Week34 Incidence per 1,000 Population at risk– Week35

    This week, 225,275 malaria cases were reported; an increase from 218,357 reported in week 34. Malaria incidence increased from 4.8 to 4.96 cases per 1,000 population at risk. Obongi, Lamwo, Pader, Agago, Adjumani, and Terego dis-

    tricts recorded the highest malaria incidence (over 20/1000 population) this week. High incidence rates (12-20 per 1,000

    population at risk) were observed in Dokolo, Kibuku, Kitgum, Madi-Okollo and Omoro districts. The number of malaria deaths increased from 23 in week 34 to 25 in week 35. Deaths were reported in 12 districts including; Mbarara-9, Butalejja-2, Gulu-2, Kyenjojo-2, Butambala-1, Hoima-1, Isingiro-1, etc.

    SARI, TB, MEASLES AND CHOLERA CASES

    There was an increase in the number of Measles cases from 50 in week 34 to 269 in the current week. Cases were reported in 14 districts, with Wakiso accounting for over 90% of the total i.e. (245/269).

    Severe Acute Respiratory Infections (SARI) increased from 77 cases in week 34 to 91 in week 35 with 68% of these reported in Kampala, Wakiso and Buvuma.

    Compared with week 34, there was a decrease in new and relapse TB cases. Majority of the TB cases were reported from Kampala (86), Wakiso (57) , Oyam(48) and Fort Portal (21). 128 Districts reported at least a TB case. Two cases of MDR TB were reported.

    REPORTING RATES

    The reporting rate reduced to 85.7% from 86.3% in week 34. The reporting rates have been over 80% the last 9 weeks except in week 32.
